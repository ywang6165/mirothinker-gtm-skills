# Competitor Engagement Sequence

For leads sourced from `competitor-post-engagers`. These leads engaged with a competitor's LinkedIn content — they're aware of the problem space and actively following solutions. Handle with care: don't trash-talk the competitor.

**Available personalization variables:**
- `{first_name}`, `{last_name}`, `{company}`, `{title}`, `{industry}`
- `{competitor_name}` — the competitor whose post they engaged with
- `{post_topic}` — what the competitor's post was about
- `{engagement_type}` — "commented" or "reacted"
- `{comment_snippet}` — their comment text (if commenter)

---

## Step 1: Connection Request (300 chars max)

### Variant A — Topic-focused (don't mention competitor)
```
{first_name} — saw you engaging with content about {post_topic}. We're working in the same space with a different angle. Would enjoy connecting.
```

### Variant B — Industry peer
```
Hey {first_name}, noticed we're both deep in the {post_topic} world. Always good to connect with {industry} folks thinking about this. Let's connect.
```

### Variant C — For commenters
```
{first_name} — your thoughts on {post_topic} were sharp. We see the same patterns. Would be great to connect.
```

**Important:** Do NOT mention the competitor by name in the connection request. Lead with the topic, not the competitor.

---

## Step 2: Follow-up 1 — Differentiated Value (Day 3)

### Variant A — Different approach framing
```
Thanks for connecting, {first_name}. I noticed you've been following the {post_topic} conversation closely.

Most solutions in this space take a [competitor's approach description]. We've found that [your differentiated approach] tends to work better for teams that [specific use case].

Is {post_topic} something {company} is actively evaluating, or more of a watch-and-wait?
```

### Variant B — Curiosity-based
```
Appreciate the connection. Quick question — when you were looking at {post_topic} content, was that more out of curiosity or is {company} actively exploring solutions?

No wrong answer. Just want to make sure I'm sharing relevant stuff if we keep chatting.
```

### Variant C — For commenters with rich context
```
Thanks for connecting. Your comment about {post_topic} stuck with me — "{comment_snippet}"

We hear that a lot from {industry} teams. The tricky part is most tools solve half the problem. Curious if that's been your experience at {company}?
```

---

## Step 3: Follow-up 2 — Social Proof (Day 7)

### Variant A — Competitive displacement story
```
{first_name}, thought this might be relevant: a {industry} company recently switched their {post_topic} approach. They were using [general category, not competitor name] and hitting [specific limitation].

After the switch, they saw [specific metric improvement]. Happy to walk through what changed — takes about 15 minutes.
```

### Variant B — Side-by-side framing
```
One thing I've been hearing from {title}s in {industry}: the {post_topic} tools they evaluated all look similar on the surface, but the differences show up in [specific area — implementation, scale, support, etc.].

If {company} is comparing options, I can share what questions to ask. No pitch — just the framework we've seen work.
```

---

## Step 4: Follow-up 3 — Breakup (Day 14)

### Variant A
```
{first_name} — I'll leave this here: if {company} ever evaluates {post_topic} solutions, I'd love a seat at the table. If you've already sorted it out, glad to hear it.

Enjoy the connection either way.
```

### Variant B
```
Last message on this from me. If {post_topic} becomes a priority for {company}, happy to be a resource. No hard feelings if it's not the right time.
```

---

## Step 5: InMail — Standalone (Day 7, if connection not accepted)

### Subject (200 chars max)
**Variant A:** `{post_topic} — a different take for {company}`
**Variant B:** `How {industry} teams are rethinking {post_topic}`
**Variant C:** `{first_name}, quick question about {post_topic}`

### Body (1,900 chars max)

**Variant A:**
```
Hi {first_name},

I noticed you've been engaging with content about {post_topic} on LinkedIn — seems like it's on your radar.

Wanted to reach out because we take a different approach than what you've probably seen so far. Most {post_topic} solutions focus on [competitor's typical approach]. We've found that [your differentiated approach] delivers better results for {industry} companies, especially when [specific scenario].

A couple of quick proof points:
- [Company type] saw [specific improvement] after switching approaches
- [Company type] reduced [specific metric] by [X]% in [timeframe]

If {company} is exploring options — or even just thinking about it — a quick conversation could save your team a lot of evaluation time. I can share exactly what to look for and what pitfalls to avoid.

Worth 15 minutes?

Best,
[Sender name]
```

**Variant B — Softer approach:**
```
Hi {first_name},

I saw you reacting to posts about {post_topic} and wanted to share something that might be useful.

We've been working with {industry} companies on this challenge, and there's a common pattern: teams evaluate the obvious solutions, pick one, and then realize 6 months later that [specific limitation] is a bigger problem than they expected.

The teams that avoid this tend to ask three questions upfront:
1. [Question that highlights your advantage]
2. [Question about a common gotcha]
3. [Question about long-term scalability]

If this is relevant to {company}, happy to share more context. If the timing isn't right, no worries at all.

Best,
[Sender name]
```
