# Event Attendee Sequence

For leads sourced from `luma-event-attendees` or `get-qualified-leads-from-luma`. These leads attended or registered for a relevant industry event — they're investing time in the topic, which signals interest and awareness.

**Strategy:** The event is shared context. Use it to build rapport, reference specific sessions or themes, and position yourself as part of the same community.

**Available personalization variables:**
- `{first_name}`, `{last_name}`, `{company}`, `{title}`, `{industry}`
- `{event_name}` — the event they attended/registered for
- `{event_topic}` — the main theme of the event
- `{event_date}` — when the event happened
- `{speaker_name}` — a notable speaker at the event (if known)

---

## Step 1: Connection Request (300 chars max)

### Variant A — Event reference
```
{first_name} — saw you were at {event_name}. Great event. We're working on {event_topic} from the tooling side. Would enjoy connecting.
```

### Variant B — Shared community
```
Hey {first_name}, fellow {event_name} attendee here. Always good to connect with {industry} people investing time in {event_topic}. Let's connect.
```

### Variant C — Speaker reference
```
{first_name} — were you at {speaker_name}'s session at {event_name}? Their take on {event_topic} was sharp. We're building in that space. Let's connect.
```

---

## Step 2: Follow-up 1 — Event Debrief (Day 3)

### Variant A — Key takeaway
```
Thanks for connecting, {first_name}. What was your biggest takeaway from {event_name}?

Mine was [specific insight or theme from the event]. It's something we've been seeing play out with {industry} companies — the gap between knowing about {event_topic} and actually implementing it is wider than people think.

Curious how {company} is thinking about this.
```

### Variant B — Build on a session
```
Appreciate the connection. {event_name} had some solid content. The session on {event_topic} in particular hit close to home for what we do.

One thing that didn't get enough airtime: [your unique angle on the topic]. It's the part that {industry} teams tend to underestimate.

Is {event_topic} on {company}'s roadmap, or more of a future consideration?
```

### Variant C — Community-building
```
Thanks for connecting, {first_name}. I've been connecting with a few people from {event_name} — it's turning into a good group of {industry} folks thinking about {event_topic}.

If you're interested, happy to intro you to a couple of the sharper people in the group. No catch — just good networking.
```

---

## Step 3: Follow-up 2 — Social Proof (Day 7)

### Variant A — Post-event momentum
```
{first_name}, a lot of {industry} leaders came out of {event_name} wanting to act on {event_topic}. The ones actually making progress tend to start with [specific approach].

We've helped {N} companies do exactly that. Want to hear how one of them went from "interested" to "live" in [timeframe]?
```

### Variant B — Practical follow-through
```
Most people leave events inspired and do nothing. The few who actually execute on {event_topic} tend to share one trait: they find a partner who's done it before.

We've been that partner for {N} {industry} companies. If {company} wants to turn the {event_name} insights into results, I can show you the fastest path. 15 minutes.
```

---

## Step 4: Follow-up 3 — Breakup (Day 14)

### Variant A
```
{first_name} — figured the post-{event_name} energy might have faded by now. If {event_topic} comes back around as a priority, I'm here. Enjoy the connection.
```

### Variant B
```
Last note from me. {event_name} was weeks ago, and I know priorities shift. If {event_topic} is still on the list for {company}, let's talk. If not, no worries.
```

---

## Step 5: InMail — Standalone (Day 7, if connection not accepted)

### Subject (200 chars max)
**Variant A:** `Fellow {event_name} attendee — relevant to {company}`
**Variant B:** `{event_topic}: turning {event_name} insights into action`
**Variant C:** `{first_name}, following up from {event_name}`

### Body (1,900 chars max)

**Variant A:**
```
Hi {first_name},

I noticed you were at {event_name} — great event. The discussions around {event_topic} were particularly relevant to what we do.

I'm reaching out because we've helped {industry} companies take the concepts discussed at events like this and turn them into operational reality. The gap between "this is interesting" and "this is live in our workflow" is where most teams get stuck.

Here's what we've seen work:
- [Company type] implemented {event_topic} in [timeframe] and saw [result]
- [Company type] started with a [small scope] and expanded to [full scope] after proving ROI

If {company} is looking to act on what you learned at {event_name}, I can share the playbook that's worked for companies in your space. Takes about 15 minutes.

Worth a conversation?

Best,
[Sender name]
```

**Variant B — Lighter touch:**
```
Hi {first_name},

I saw you attended {event_name}. I was there too (virtually/in person), and the {event_topic} content was solid.

One thing I've noticed: about 80% of event attendees are "interested" in {event_topic}, but only about 10% actually implement something within 90 days. The difference usually comes down to having a clear starting point.

We help {industry} companies find that starting point. If {company} is in the "interested but not sure where to start" camp, I've got a quick framework that might help.

No pitch — just the framework. Interested?

Best,
[Sender name]
```
