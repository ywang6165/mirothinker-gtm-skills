# Qualification Prompt: AI Event Attendees — GTM Engineering

Generated: 2026-02-22

## Campaign Context
- **Product:** GTM engineering agency + platform — builds outbound sales systems, content, SEO, launches, and ads for startups
- **Campaign Angle:** Targeting founders and GTM leaders attending AI developer events in SF for potential agency/platform engagement
- **Problem Solved:** Companies need leads and revenue but lack the engineering and systems to run outbound sales, content, and growth at scale

## Hard Disqualifiers (Instant No)
- **Agencies or consultancies** — competitors, not prospects
- **Pre-seed / idea-stage** — no budget, too early
- **Pure IC engineer / researcher / data scientist** — no buying authority for GTM services (unless also a founder/CTO)
- **Non-US company** — US-based companies only
- **Organization accounts** — not a person (e.g., "Google DeepMind" as an attendee)

## Hard Qualifiers (Instant Yes)
- **Founder/CEO at a seed-to-Series B US startup** — core ICP
- **CMO, CRO, VP Sales, VP Marketing, Head of Growth at a US startup** — direct buyer
- **Chief of Staff at a US startup** — often owns GTM operations

## Company Criteria
| Criterion | Qualified | Disqualified | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|-------|
| Size/Stage | Seed to Series B (flexible upward) | Pre-seed, idea-stage | Larger companies (Series C+, public) are OK but not ideal |
| Industry | Any B2B or B2C startup | Agencies, consultancies, marketing firms | Competitors are out |
| Geography | US-based | Non-US | Must be a US company |

## Person Criteria
| Criterion | Qualified | Disqualified | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|-------|
| Titles | Founder, Co-Founder, CEO, CTO, CMO, CRO, COO, Chief of Staff, VP Sales, VP Marketing, VP Growth, Head of Sales, Head of Growth, Head of Marketing, Director of Sales/Marketing/Growth, Growth Lead, Sales Manager, Marketing Director | Engineer (IC), Researcher, Data Scientist, Designer, Student, Intern | CTO/technical founder qualifies; pure IC engineers do not |

| Department | Sales, Marketing, Growth, Revenue, BizDev, Partnerships, General Management | Engineering (IC), Research, Design, HR, Legal | Exception: founders/CTOs regardless of department |

## Behavioral & Situational Signals
**Positive signals (boost qualification):**
- Bio mentions building a company, launching a product, or scaling
- Recently raised funding
- Active on LinkedIn/Twitter discussing growth, sales, or marketing topics
- Attending events focused on business/growth (not purely technical research)
- "Aspiring founder" or "building something" with GTM context

**Negative signals (reduce qualification):**
- Bio is purely technical/research focused with no business context
- Student or academic without a startup
- Bio mentions freelancing or consulting (potential competitor)

## Confidence Rules
- **High Confidence:** Title and company are clearly identifiable. Can determine stage, geography, and role.
- **Medium Confidence:** Have name and some bio/title info but missing company details (size, stage, geography). Title suggests fit.
- **Low Confidence:** Minimal data — no bio, no title, no company info. Can only guess based on event role or name. Still make a yes/no call but flag for manual review.

## Edge Case Guidance
- "Aspiring founder" at a university → Low confidence, likely No unless they have an active startup
- CTO at a startup → Yes, technical founders qualify as buyers
- "Host" at an event community (e.g., "Host @ AI Nexus") → Depends on whether they also run a startup; if community organizer only, likely No
- Someone "building something in AI" with no company → Low confidence, lean Yes if other signals suggest founder
- Someone at a large company (Google, Meta) → No unless they're clearly doing something entrepreneurial on the side

## Qualification Reasoning Instructions
When evaluating a lead, structure your reasoning as:
1. Check hard disqualifiers first — if any match, immediately disqualify.
2. Check hard qualifiers — if any match, lean strongly toward qualifying.
3. Evaluate company criteria against thresholds.
4. Evaluate person criteria against thresholds.
5. Factor in behavioral/situational signals as tiebreakers.
6. Assign confidence based on data completeness.
7. Write 2-3 sentence reasoning summarizing the decision.
