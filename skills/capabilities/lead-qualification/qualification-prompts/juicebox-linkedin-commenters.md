# Qualification Prompt: Juicebox LinkedIn Commenters

Generated: 2026-02-25

## Campaign Context
- **Product:** Juicebox (PeopleGPT) — AI-powered talent sourcing platform. Natural language search across 800M+ profiles from 60+ data sources. AI agents that source, screen, and engage candidates 24/7.
- **Campaign Angle:** Intercept active LinkedIn participants who are engaging with talent sourcing/recruiting content — they've signaled pain or active interest in sourcing tools/methods.
- **Problem Solved:** Manual and keyword-based candidate sourcing is slow and misses non-obvious candidates. PeopleGPT lets recruiters describe what they want in plain English and finds candidates that Boolean search can't.

---

## Hard Disqualifiers (Instant No)

- **Job seekers:** Their LinkedIn headline or current role shows they're an individual contributor looking for work (e.g., "Open to opportunities", "Seeking new role"). They don't buy recruiting tools.
- **Student / intern:** Title suggests they're in school or early career with no hiring authority.
- **Competitor employee:** Works at SeekOut, hireEZ, Findem, Eightfold AI, LinkedIn (Microsoft), HireQuotient, Fetcher, Gem, or any direct AI sourcing tool. They won't buy.
- **Pure vendor/consultant selling recruiting services:** Their business is staffing/headhunting as a revenue model — not a platform buyer. (Note: independent recruiters who *use* tools ARE prospects.)
- **No clear connection to recruiting/talent:** Title and company have no relationship to hiring, HR, talent, or people operations. Comment was likely off-topic engagement.
- **Company size 1 (solo founder, no team):** Cannot be a platform buyer with meaningful recurring revenue potential.

---

## Hard Qualifiers (Instant Yes — Lean Strongly Toward Qualifying)

- **Head of Talent / Director of Talent Acquisition / VP of TA** at a company with 50+ employees: Core buyer, has budget, has pain.
- **Recruiting agency owner or lead recruiter** who commented asking for tool recommendations: Actively shopping.
- **Founder/CEO at a startup (10-200 employees) actively hiring:** High urgency, will try self-serve immediately.
- **Comment explicitly mentions frustration with LinkedIn Recruiter cost, Boolean search limits, or data gaps:** Pain is expressed — perfect moment to reach out.
- **Comment asks "what sourcing tools do you use?" or similar:** Active evaluation signal.

## Behavioral & Situational Signals

### Signals That Boost Qualification Score
- Comment text expresses frustration with current sourcing tools (especially LinkedIn Recruiter)
- Comment asks for tool recommendations, alternatives, or benchmarks
- Comment shares a recent sourcing win — they're an active practitioner
- Company is currently hiring (detectable via LinkedIn job postings or recent funding announcement)
- Company recently raised funding (Series A/B = hiring spree incoming)
- Commenter has posted their own content about recruiting/sourcing (shows active practitioner)
- Company in AI/ML space — Juicebox's sweet spot customer type

### Signals That Reduce Qualification Score
- Comment is purely academic/theoretical (no practical pain implied)
- Commenter is clearly a vendor promoting their own product in the comments
- Company appears to be in a hiring freeze (no open roles, recent layoff news)
- Comment is a one-word reaction ("Great post!", "Agreed") — low intent signal
- Commenter is at a company with a known enterprise ATS/sourcing contract that would block adoption

---

## Confidence Rules

- **High Confidence:** Enriched profile data available with clear title, company size visible or inferable, AND at least one qualifying signal (title match + company stage/size fit).
- **Medium Confidence:** Title matches but company size is unclear, or company size fits but title is borderline (e.g., "HR Manager" vs "TA Manager").
- **Low Confidence:** No enrichment data, profile is sparse, title is ambiguous, or company is unrecognizable. Still make a Yes/No call but flag for manual review.

---

## Edge Case Guidance

- **"Talent Acquisition Partner"** — Qualifies. This is a standard IC recruiter title at mid-large companies with real sourcing work.
- **"People Operations Manager"** — Qualifies if company is <200 employees (likely does own sourcing). Borderline for 200+ (probably not hands-on sourcing).
- **"Executive Recruiter"** at a search firm — Qualifies. Search firms are a core market for Juicebox.
- **Freelance/contract recruiter** — Qualifies. Often the most price-sensitive but also most willing to try new tools quickly.
- **"Talent Sourcer"** — High-intent qualifier. These are the exact users of PeopleGPT.
- **Founder at a 5-person startup** — Borderline. Qualify if there's hiring intent signal. They're often self-serve customers who convert quickly.
- **CHRO at 10,000+ company** — Technically qualifies on title but likely too enterprise for PLG. Qualify but note as enterprise target (needs sales touch, not self-serve).
- **Comment was just a "like" or emoji** — Extract the person but reduce confidence. Their engagement was passive, not active.

## Qualification Reasoning Instructions

When evaluating a lead, structure your reasoning as:

1. **Check hard disqualifiers first** — if any match, immediately disqualify with single-line reason.
2. **Check hard qualifiers** — if any match, lean strongly toward qualifying (note the qualifier).
3. **Evaluate company criteria** — size, industry, stage against thresholds.
4. **Evaluate person criteria** — title, seniority, department match.
5. **Factor in behavioral signals** — what did they actually say in the comment? What does that reveal about their pain/intent?
6. **Assign confidence** based on data completeness (enriched profile vs. sparse data).
7. **Write 2-3 sentence reasoning** covering: title/company fit, intent signal from the comment, and any notable qualifiers or flags.

**Example reasoning (qualified):**
> "Sarah is Director of Talent Acquisition at a 150-person Series B SaaS company — squarely in Juicebox's ICP. Her comment asked 'anyone using AI tools beyond LinkedIn Recruiter for technical sourcing?' which is a direct buying signal. High confidence given enriched profile data and explicit tool-evaluation intent."

**Example reasoning (disqualified):**
> "Marcus is an HR Generalist at a 2,000-person manufacturing company — no indication of sourcing tool ownership and company vertical is a poor fit. His comment was a generic 'great point!' with no sourcing-specific content. Disqualified on role scope and vertical."
